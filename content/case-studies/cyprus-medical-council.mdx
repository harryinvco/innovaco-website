## The Challenge

The Cyprus Medical Council maintained their doctor registry through a combination of spreadsheets and internal databases. When the public, hospitals, or insurance companies needed to verify a doctor's registration status or find a specialist, the process involved phone calls and manual lookups by council staff.

The registry needed to be publicly accessible, searchable, bilingual, and hosted in a way that met the council's data governance requirements.

## What We Built

A Next.js web platform with three main interfaces:

**Public search** — anyone can search the registry by doctor name, medical specialty, or registration number. Results show registration status, specialty, and practice information. The interface works in both Greek and English, with content served in the user's selected language.

**Verification portal** — hospitals and insurance companies can verify a doctor's registration status and credentials through a dedicated verification flow. This replaced the phone-based verification process that was consuming staff time.

**Admin panel** — council staff manage the registry through a secure admin interface. They can add new registrations, update statuses, manage specialty categories, and generate reports. The admin panel includes audit logging for compliance requirements.

The platform is hosted on EU infrastructure with no data leaving the European Union. Authentication uses role-based access control to separate public, verification, and administrative access.

## The Result

The registry is now publicly accessible for the first time. Verification that previously required a phone call now takes seconds. Council staff have a modern admin interface instead of spreadsheets. The bilingual implementation means both Greek-speaking and English-speaking users can access the information they need without friction.
