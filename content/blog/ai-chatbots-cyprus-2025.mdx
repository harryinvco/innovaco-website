## The problem nobody talks about

Walk into any business in Limassol, Larnaca, or Nicosia after 6pm and try to get a question answered. Call the number on the website. Fill in the contact form. Send a WhatsApp message.

Nothing happens until tomorrow morning.

For most Cypriot SMEs, customer support ends when the office closes. Enquiries from tourists planning a trip, patients looking for a clinic, or potential customers comparing options get no response for 12-16 hours. By the time staff arrive the next morning, many of those leads have already chosen a competitor.

An AI chatbot fixes this. Not with a generic "we'll get back to you" auto-reply, but with a system that actually understands the business, speaks the customer's language, and gives useful answers in real time.

## What a chatbot actually is (and is not)

When we say AI chatbot, we do not mean the frustrating decision trees that make you press 1 for sales, 2 for support, then ask you to repeat yourself three times. Those are rule-based bots from a decade ago.

A modern AI chatbot uses something called RAG, which stands for Retrieval-Augmented Generation. In plain terms: you give the system your business documents, your FAQs, your product catalogue, and your policies. When a customer asks a question, the AI searches through all of that knowledge and generates an answer based on your real information.

The customer asks in Greek, the bot answers in Greek. They switch to English mid-sentence, the bot switches too. This is not translation. The AI genuinely handles both languages because the underlying models understand them.

## Why WhatsApp changes everything in Cyprus

In most European markets, chatbots live on websites. In Cyprus, they need to live on WhatsApp.

WhatsApp is the default communication channel for Cypriot businesses. Customers send a message to a business the same way they message a friend. Hotel bookings, restaurant reservations, appointment requests, product enquiries: in Cyprus, these happen on WhatsApp first.

This means a chatbot that only works as a website widget is solving half the problem. The real value comes when your AI handles WhatsApp conversations natively. The customer messages your business number, the AI responds with real information, and if it cannot handle the query, it escalates to a staff member with full context.

## What it costs and how long it takes

A production chatbot for a Cypriot SME typically costs between €1,200 and €3,000 for the initial build. This includes knowledge base setup, bilingual configuration, WhatsApp integration, and deployment on EU-hosted infrastructure.

The timeline is 2-3 weeks from kickoff to live deployment. The first week is knowledge building: we audit your existing documents, FAQ sheets, menus, catalogues, and policies. The second week is configuration and testing. The third week is staff training and go-live.

Monthly running costs depend on the volume but are typically €50-€150 for hosting and AI API usage. There is no per-seat fee and no license to renew.

## The bilingual requirement

Building a chatbot for the Cyprus market without Greek support is not an option. Your customers will message in Greek. If the bot responds in English, the experience breaks immediately.

But bolting on a translation layer creates awkward, unnatural responses. The bot needs to understand Greek natively, including Cypriot dialect patterns, common abbreviations, and the mix of Greeklish that many younger customers use.

This is why we build on models that handle Greek and English simultaneously, not a translate-then-respond pipeline. The customer should never feel like they are talking to a translation tool.

## What can go wrong

Three things break chatbots in practice.

**Bad knowledge base.** If you feed the bot outdated menus, wrong prices, or contradictory information, it will confidently give wrong answers. The knowledge base needs to be accurate and maintained.

**No escalation path.** The bot should know what it does not know. When a customer asks something outside its scope, it needs to hand off to a human smoothly, not loop in circles. Every bot we build has a clear escalation trigger.

**Ignoring it after launch.** A chatbot is not a set-and-forget tool. You need to review conversations weekly for the first month, identify gaps in the knowledge base, and update information when prices change, menus update, or policies shift.

## When NOT to use a chatbot

Honesty matters here. A chatbot is not the right solution for every business.

If your business handles fewer than 10 customer enquiries per week, the investment may not make sense. If your product or service requires deep consultative selling where every conversation is unique, a chatbot will frustrate more than it helps. If you are not willing to maintain the knowledge base, the bot will degrade over time.

The best candidates are businesses with high enquiry volume, repetitive questions, after-hours demand, and a clear knowledge base to draw from. Hotels, clinics, professional associations, restaurants, and e-commerce businesses are the sweet spot.
