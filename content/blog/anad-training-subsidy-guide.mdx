## What most employers do not know

Every employer in Cyprus who contributes to the Human Resource Development Authority (ANAD) levy is entitled to claim back a significant portion of their training costs. The subsidy can cover up to 80% of approved training expenses, including AI and digital transformation programmes.

Yet the majority of Cypriot employers never claim. Some do not know the scheme exists. Others assume the paperwork is too complicated. Many believe it only applies to large companies or traditional vocational training.

None of that is true.

## Who qualifies

If your business is registered in the Republic of Cyprus and pays the ANAD levy (0.5% of payroll, deducted automatically via social insurance contributions), you qualify. This includes sole traders, SMEs, and large employers. There is no minimum company size.

The scheme covers employees on your payroll. Contract workers and freelancers are generally not eligible, though there are exceptions for certain types of engagement.

## How the subsidy works, step by step

The ANAD process has five stages. It is not complicated, but it does require filing paperwork at the right times.

**1. Select an approved programme.** The training programme must be delivered by an ANAD-approved provider (we are one). The programme must have a clear learning plan, defined outcomes, and meet ANAD's quality standards.

**2. Submit a pre-approval application.** Before the training takes place, you submit form AIT 1/2 to ANAD with the programme details, participant list, dates, and costs. ANAD reviews and approves the application. This step is critical: training without pre-approval is not eligible for subsidy.

**3. Deliver the training.** The approved programme runs as planned. Attendance is recorded. Participants complete any required assessments. We handle all of this on our end.

**4. Submit the claim.** After the training is complete, you submit form AIT 4 with attendance records, receipts, and completion certificates. The claim must be submitted within specific deadlines, typically within 4 months of the training date.

**5. Receive the refund.** ANAD processes the claim and credits the subsidy amount to the employer. Typical processing time is 2-4 months after submission.

## Common mistakes to avoid

**Missing the pre-approval deadline.** Training must be approved before it happens. Submitting after the fact means no subsidy. We submit pre-approval applications at least 3 weeks before each programme.

**Incomplete attendance records.** ANAD requires proper documentation of who attended, for how long, and what they completed. We maintain detailed records for every session.

**Wrong cost allocation.** The subsidy covers direct training costs but has specific rules about what counts. Venue hire, travel, and accommodation have separate limits. We ensure claims are structured correctly.

**Forgetting to claim.** The most common mistake is simply not filing the paperwork after the training is done. Because we handle the claim submission, this does not happen with our programmes.

## The real numbers

To put this in perspective: a half-day AI for Business Owners programme costs €150-€250 per participant. With the ANAD subsidy, your net cost drops to €30-€50 per person. A full corporate in-house training day at €1,500-€3,000 becomes €300-€600 after subsidy.

For a team of 10 people attending a full-day programme, the difference between claiming and not claiming could be €2,000 or more.
